Mitchell — Farm Equipment & Machinery Advisory

Don't buy the wrong gear. Get a second opinion first.

Farming equipment is one of the biggest expenses a farm will make. The wrong purchase can cost tens of thousands of dollars in lost productivity, repairs and resale losses. Mitchell has worked in shearing and wheat farming across central Western Australia and brings real hands-on knowledge of what works, what doesn't, and what to look out for before you sign anything.

01

Pre-Purchase Equipment Inspections

Before you commit to buying a piece of machinery or farm equipment, get Mitchell to look it over. He knows what wear patterns to look for, what gets hidden before a sale, and whether the price reflects the real condition of the machine.

02

Machinery Performance Evaluation

Already own equipment that isn't performing the way it should? Mitchell can evaluate whether the issue is mechanical, operational or simply the wrong tool for the job — before you spend money on repairs that won't fix the real problem.

03

Suitability Assessments

Not every machine suits every operation. Mitchell assesses whether a piece of equipment actually fits your farm, your terrain, your crop or your livestock setup — so you are not buying something that looks right on paper but doesn't work in practice.

04

Durability, Usability & Maintenance Advice

Some equipment is built to last. Some isn't. Mitchell gives you an honest read on long-term durability, day-to-day usability and what the real maintenance costs are likely to be — the things a dealer won't always tell you.

05

Second Opinion Before Major Purchases

About to spend $50,000 or more on a piece of equipment? Get a second opinion from someone with no financial interest in whether you buy or not. Mitchell gives you an independent, practical assessment so you can make the decision with confidence.

06

Combined with Sharon's Financial Analysis

Mitchell's equipment advice pairs directly with Sharon's financial analysis. Together they can tell you not just whether the machine is worth buying — but whether the purchase makes financial sense for your operation right now.
